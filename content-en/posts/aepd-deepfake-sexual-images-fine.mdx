**AEPD Fine for Sexual Deepfake Dissemination with AI: Everything You Need to Know**

**Ricardo Scarpa (derechoartificial.com)**

*The Spanish Data Protection Agency sanctions for the first time the dissemination of false intimate images generated with artificial intelligence. The case involves minors and sets a key precedent regarding deepfakes and GDPR.*

## **What Happened? The Case in Three Key Points**

The Spanish Data Protection Agency (AEPD) has resolved a sanctioning proceeding—EXP202503445—against a person who participated in the dissemination of manipulated images with artificial intelligence. The images associated the real faces of persons (with evidence that among them there were minors) with nude bodies that did not belong to them. The images were shared in messaging groups and were even published on platforms like OnlyFans and pornographic pages.

The offender, identified as A.A.A. (anonymized name in the public resolution), is a minor. His parents, as legal representatives, proceeded to acknowledge responsibility and voluntarily pay the fine: €1,200, resulting from applying two 20% reductions on the base fine of €2,000 set by the AEPD.

## **Why Is This Case Important?**

- **First AEPD sanction for sexual deepfakes with AI**: Sets precedent regarding GDPR application to this type of content.

- **Confirms that deepfakes constitute personal data processing**: Although the body is false, the real face is personal data protected by law.

- **Dissemination—not merely creation—generates liability**: Sharing these images in a messaging group is sufficient to be sanctioned.

- **Affects minors both as victims and offenders**: The AEPD does not exempt from sanction due to age and activates enhanced protection mechanisms.

## **What Law Was Applied?**

The AEPD has applied Article 6.1 of the European Union General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR), which requires that all personal data processing have a legitimate legal basis. In this case—as in all non-consensual sexual deepfakes—no basis exists to justify the processing: neither consent, nor contract, nor legal obligation, nor anything else.

The violation is classified as very serious (Article 72.1 of the Spanish Data Protection Act - LOPDGDD), with a three-year statute of limitations. The maximum possible sanction would be up to €20 million, although the AEPD set a very reduced amount considering the circumstances of the case (particularly the minor age of the offender and their status as an individual without business activity).

## **If You Are a Victim of a Deepfake, What Can You Do?**

1. **File a complaint with the AEPD**: You can do so through the Agency's electronic headquarters. The AEPD will investigate and, where applicable, sanction those responsible.

2. **Request removal of the images**: You have the right to demand that platforms remove the images under the "right to be forgotten" or right to erasure under Article 17 of the GDPR.

3. **Consider filing a criminal complaint**: Dissemination of intimate images without consent may also constitute a criminal offense. The administrative and criminal routes are compatible.

4. **Claim compensation**: Payment of the fine by the offender does not extinguish your right to be compensated for damages caused. You may exercise it before civil courts.

## **What Happens If You Have Disseminated a Deepfake?**

If you have participated in the creation or dissemination of sexually manipulated images without the consent of the affected person, you may face an administrative fine from the AEPD (which can reach up to €20 million, although in cases involving individuals it is usually much lower), a criminal complaint by the victim, a civil claim for damages, and the obligation to delete all images under your control.

Being a minor does not exempt you from these consequences in the administrative sphere. The AEPD can sanction you through your legal representatives.

**Is a deepfake illegal in Spain?** Yes. Dissemination of AI-manipulated sexual images without the consent of the affected person violates the GDPR and may also constitute a criminal offense.

**Can the AEPD sanction a minor?** Yes. The LOPDGDD does not provide for exemption by reason of age. The sanction is managed through the minor's legal representatives.

**Is sharing a deepfake in a WhatsApp group sanctionable?** Yes, according to the AEPD. Participating in dissemination through messaging groups can make you liable for unlawful data processing.
