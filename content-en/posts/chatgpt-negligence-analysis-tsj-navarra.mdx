## Executive Summary

This analysis examines Auto 2/2024 from the TSJ of Navarra (Superior Court of Justice of Navarre), a pioneering resolution addressing the inclusion of non-existent provisions from the Colombian Criminal Code in Spanish proceedings. The incident, arising from the unsupervised use of generative artificial intelligence (ChatGPT), constitutes a severe reminder of the non-delegable duty of diligence and the procedural consequences of dereliction of duty in legal validation.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 2. Context and Scope of the Document

Auto 2/2024 should not be interpreted as a mere procedural incident due to clerical error, but as a cutting-edge milestone that defines the integrity of the judicial rite and the lex artis ad hoc in the digital era. The resolution positions human supervision as an inviolable ethical imperative, warning that the adoption of technological tools cannot justify the violation of loyalty and probity principles.

## Anatomy of the Gross Clerical Error

The conflict crystallized when the Chamber detected in the complaint the citation of a non-existent Article 454B, accompanied by the expression "medio cognoscitivo" (cognitive means), terminology alien to Spanish procedural lexicon. The AI's "finding," which the attorney incorporated without review, read verbatim:

"He who, to prevent it from being used as a cognitive means during investigation, or as evidence in trial, conceals, alters, or destroys material evidence of those mentioned in the Criminal Procedure Code, shall incur in..."

This wording, actually belonging to the legislation of the Republic of Colombia, was fallaciously presented as doctrine from the Second Chamber of the Spanish Supreme Court.

## "So What?" Impact Analysis

The false attribution of interpretations to the judicial leadership is not a simple typo; it represents manifest recklessness. This act undermines institutional trust and causes unnecessary waste of public resources, forcing the court to verify non-existent norms, which immediately triggers sanctioning powers to preserve the probity of the procedural rite.

Connector: This breach of legitimate trust finds its technical response in the control mechanisms provided in civil procedural law.

## 3. Legal Foundations

Article 247 of the Civil Procedure Act (LEC) constitutes the strategic bastion for defending process integrity against abuse of right and bad faith. Its application allows the court to react to conduct that deviates from the ethical standards required of legal operators.

| Article 247 LEC Section | Definition and Scope |
|------------------------|---------------------|
| Section 1: Good Faith | Imperative to conduct all procedural acts with loyalty and probity. |
| Section 3: Sanctioning Power | Court's authority to correct bad faith conduct through fines. |
| Proportionality Criteria | Graduable amounts between €180 and €6,000, depending on severity and harm. |

The opening of a "separate file for procedural bad faith" is a deontological control mechanism allowing the court to sanction the attorney's conduct without contaminating or delaying the resolution on the merits. This tool guarantees that responsibility for technical negligence falls on the professional without prejudicing the client's right to effective judicial protection.

## "So What?" Impact Analysis

The sanctioning power does not seek to censure innovation but to preserve procedural loyalty. In this case, the court acts as custodian of legal truth, contrasting negligence in technical supervision with the non-delegable duty of veracity assumed by every participant.

Connector: To understand how a professional could incur such a blunder, it is necessary to analyze the intrinsic pathologies of the technology employed.

## 4. Legal and Regulatory Keys

The phenomenon of "legal hallucinations"—the generation of false data with irreproachable technical verisimilitude—represents a systemic risk for contemporary administration of justice.

## Technical Risks in Language Models (LLM)

Matthew Dahl's study (2024) reveals a critical prevalence of fictitious data in automated legal research:

* 69% error rate in ChatGPT 3.5.
* 88% error rate in Llama 2.

These systems lack self-awareness and factual validation capability; they operate through statistical predictions of text sequences, persisting in error even under counterfactual questioning.

## "So What?" Impact Analysis

Human supervision is not a good practice recommendation, but, according to the Council of the European Bar (CCBE), constitutes an indispensable precondition for the Rule of Law. Without the lawyer's critical filter, the judicial system is exposed to the arbitrariness of algorithms that do not understand the legal consequences of their results.

Connector: This technological reality shifts the focus of analysis to the professional's ultimate responsibility to the judicial body.

## 5. Risks and Obligations in Practice

Professional responsibility in drafting legal documents is a personal and non-delegable duty. Any dereliction of control and technical validation functions constitutes a violation of the lex artis that cannot be justified by algorithmic efficiency.

## Mitigating Factors and Resolution

The TSJ of Navarra opted to dismiss the separate file based on an evaluation of the attorney's subsequent conduct:

1. Immediate reaction: Upon detecting the anomaly, the professional presented his "most sincere apologies."
2. Remediation strategy: Request to consider the paragraph as "not stated," admitting involuntary but inadequate handling of the tool.
3. Novelty and Pedagogy: The court considered that the recent nature of AI and the public nature of the Auto served as sufficient warning for the professional community.

## "So What?" Impact Analysis

Honesty and spontaneous correction operate as mitigating factors under Art. 247 LEC. Procedural transparency is revealed as the only path to mitigate the consequences of negligence in technical supervision, reaffirming that technological error is excusable only if the professional response is ethical and diligent.

Connector: This incident yields fundamental lessons that configure the new standard of legal practice.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 6. Critical Assessment and Conclusions

Auto 2/2024 stands as an ethical warning for the entire legal community. It establishes that AI must be understood exclusively as an aid to drafting and never as a substitute for the criteria, logic, and expert knowledge of the lawyer.

## Fundamental Lessons

1. **Non-Delegable Professional Responsibility:** The lawyer is the legal author of his document. AI is an instrument; authorship and its legal consequences belong exclusively to the professional.
2. **Effective Human Supervision Duty:** Validation in official sources (Cendoj, BOE) is mandatory. "Copy-pasting" without legal verification is a renunciation of the essence of legal function.
3. **Procedural Good Faith:** Loyalty to the court requires immediate recognition of error. Transparency is the bastion that protects professional probity against technical failures.

## "So What?" Impact Analysis

The lawyer remains the ultimate guarantor of the accuracy of his arguments. Technological innovation is an ally of Law, provided it is exercised under unwavering vigilance ensuring that the cited norm is, effectively, the current norm in the forum.

Professional excellence in the twenty-first century resides in the exact balance between algorithmic efficiency and the non-delegable prudence of the lawyer.
