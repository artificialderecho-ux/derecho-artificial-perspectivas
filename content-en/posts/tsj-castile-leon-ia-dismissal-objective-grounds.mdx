# Artificial Intelligence as an Objective Ground for Dismissal: Analysis of the TSJ of Castile and León Judgment of September 15, 2025

©Ricardo Scarpa ( derechoartificial.com )

## 1. Summary

The emergence of Artificial Intelligence (AI) in the professional services market has moved beyond the theoretical realm to shape a new scenario of conflict within social jurisdiction. This analysis examines the recent ruling of the Superior Court of Justice of Castile and León, which addresses the collision between the fundamental right to job stability and freedom of enterprise in a context of disruptive technological obsolescence. The core of the dispute lies in determining whether the advanced automation and free availability of online translation tools constitute legitimate economic and productive grounds for the elimination of positions under Article 52(c) of the Workers' Statute. The court confirms the fairness of the dismissal, validating the restructuring toward a self-management model of partners as a necessary response to the structural degradation of profit margins. This ruling establishes a critical precedent on the burden of proof in contractual termination proceedings derived from digital transformation, requiring a detailed analysis of the affected economic sector.

## 2. Introduction: The Disruptive Transformation of the Translation Sector

The TSJ of Castile and León Judgment of September 15, 2025 (ECLI: ES:TSJCL:2025:3529) constitutes a milestone in the legal interpretation of technological dismissal. Its strategic relevance lies in the validation of the "profound transformation" of sectors whose operative essence is susceptible to being replicated by predictive algorithms and large language models.

In the analyzed case, the chamber notes that the free availability of high-efficiency automatic translators on the internet has eroded traditional demand. This phenomenon does not represent a cyclical crisis, but a paradigm shift in service consumption: the end client opts for digital self-service, relegating human intervention to a "post-editing" formality.

However, the court astutely identifies an economic paradox in this transition: while post-editing is presented as the "new frontier" of the sector, its adoption leads to a drastic reduction in profit margins, given that it responds to downward price pressure and fewer orders per unit of service. This degradation of the business model necessitates moving toward leaner structures, whose viability is detailed below through accounting verification of the causal link.

## 3. Analysis of Proven Facts: The Inviability of the Traditional Model

The robustness of the economic cause in objective dismissal rests on documentary accounting evidence that must be uncontroverted. In the case of the company *Traductores e Intérpretes Eurotext, S.L.*, the trial court, reinforced by a determinative **Clarification Order**, established proven facts that block any attempt at subjective review. Said Order was fundamental in rectifying Proven Fact Fourth, expressly declaring verified the amounts reflected in the dismissal letter and the effective loss of sales volume.

The financial chronology reveals an accelerated deterioration that the court describes as "unsustainable":

- **Fiscal Year 2022:** Operations in precarious equilibrium with minimal profits.
- **Fiscal Year 2023:** Incurrence of net losses totaling €10,995.27.
- **First Quarter of 2024:** Critical worsening of the situation, accumulating losses of €16,471.00 in just three months, evidencing a geometric acceleration of the financial crisis.

The causal link is direct and unambiguous: competition from automatic internet translators and AI has caused a constant decline in billing. The key points supporting inviability are:

- Persistent decline in turnover year after year.
- Ineffectiveness of the post-editing model to sustain a fixed labor cost structure against price deflation.
- Imminent risk of dissolution of the entity without a drastic reduction in the payroll.

These elements allow for normative fit under Art. 52(c) of the Workers' Statute, since it is verified that the termination measure is not a matter of convenience, but a survival imperative.

## 4. Legal Basis I: Objective Dismissal under Art. 52(c) of the Workers' Statute

In the analysis of fairness, it is necessary to separate the economic cause (negative accounting result) from the productive cause (change in service delivery methods). Under Article 52(c) of the Workers' Statute, AI acts as the root cause generating an "objectively verified" need to eliminate the position. This is not merely a scenario of future losses, but a current reality where the salary cost (€1,851.78 monthly plus prorated amounts) becomes unsustainable against revenues that have collapsed due to technological impact.

Regarding the formalities required by Art. 53 of the Workers' Statute, the judgment confirms rigorous compliance with document delivery. The court clarifies that the submission of annual accounts and economic data during the trial allows full judicial cognizance on the reality of the cause alleged in the dismissal letter, refuting the allegations of defenselessness of the worker. The decision to terminate the contract proves to be a logical and proportionate organizational measure to guarantee the minimum viability of the company.

## 5. Legal Basis II: Limits of the Appeal for Review and Evidence Assessment

The analysis of this case requires emphasizing the **extraordinary nature and limited scope of the appeal for review**. Unlike an ordinary appeal, review does not constitute a second instance allowing for a *de novo* assessment of evidence.

Pursuant to **Art. 97.2 of the Social Jurisdiction Law**, the power to assess evidence belongs exclusively to the trial judge. The Chamber forcefully rejects the appellee's claims to modify proven facts fourth, fourth bis, and fourth ter, noting serious **deficiencies in casation technique** and evident **ineptitude of the grounds**:

1. **Inclusion of value judgments:** The claim that accounts "cannot" verify losses is an inadmissible subjective consideration in a proven fact.
2. **Allegation of negative facts:** The assertion that "no documentation was delivered" was dismissed. The appeal for review does not admit a "complete examination of the proceedings" to verify an omission, and the documents cited by the appellant only proved the existence of the dismissal letter, not the absence of the rest of the documentation.
3. **Irrelevance of past benefits:** The existence of modest profits in closed fiscal years (2021-2022) is legally insignificant against the reality of current and growing losses duly verified after the Clarification Order.

## 6. Business Discretion and Employee Selection Criteria

A core aspect of the defense was the challenge of the worker's selection. The appellant alleged arbitrariness based on the existence of two other employees. However, the court validates the exercise of the *ius variandi* and the employer's directional power.

The application of the Supreme Court's unified doctrine (**STS of 10/15/2003, Rec. 1534/2002** and **STS of 10/28/2014, Rec. 2784/2013**) requires that, to demonstrate arbitrariness, specific comparative data must be provided. In this proceeding, the worker failed to verify the seniority, salary, or professional category of the other employees. Since only the appellee's category as "ESP TRADUCCIÓN IX" was on record, the court was unable to make the comparability judgment, maintaining the presumption of legitimacy of the organizational decision directed at eliminating that specific position.

## 7. Survival Strategy: Transition to the Autonomous Partner Management Model

The restructuring of the company toward a structure composed exclusively of its **two autonomous partners** should not be understood as a discretionary strategic option, but as the configuration of the **minimum viable productive unit**.

This reconfiguration responds to three survival pillars:

- **Direct assumption of residual burden:** The partners personally assume translation production and post-editing, eliminating dependence on external labor.
- **Operational equilibrium:** The elimination of fixed salary costs seeks to adjust expenses to the new reality of AI-depressed revenues.
- **Conservation measure:** The court validates this model as the only one capable of avoiding imminent closure of the society given the loss of business volume caused by free technological competition.

## 8. Conclusions: AI as a Structural Factor in the Labor Market

The TSJ of Castile and León Judgment confirms that the impact of Artificial Intelligence is a structural factor that redefines grounds for contractual termination. The three fundamental lessons of this ruling are:

1. **Legitimization of AI as root cause:** Algorithmic competition is a valid basis for verifying both the sales decline and the obsolescence of traditional productive models.
2. **Rigor in Extraordinary Appeal:** The inviolability of trial court facts, except for evident and direct documentary error, shields solid accounting conclusions.
3. **Restructuring Freedom:** The employer's right to reduce their structure to the minimum autonomous partner model to preserve activity against an insurmountable technological crisis is recognized.

Ultimately, the defense of fairness in future technological dismissals will depend on the company's ability to provide detailed accounting-expert evidence connecting digital transformation with the breakdown of the economic-financial equilibrium of the productive unit.
