## Introduction

The People's Republic of China has initiated an **unprecedented transformation of its judicial system** through massive integration of artificial intelligence. This phenomenon, conceptualized under the rubric of **"Smart Courts"**, responds to well-documented pragmatic pressures: the exponential increase in litigation volume (11.43 million cases registered in 2021) against limited specialized human resources capacity.

However, this modernization poses **fundamental legal questions** about algorithmic transparency, due process protections, and accountability in automated adjudication.

### Key Questions (IRAC)

**Principal Issue:** How can the automation of judicial processes through AI be guaranteed to protect fundamental rights to due process while optimizing system efficiency?

---

## Ideological Evolution of Digital Justice

### 1.1 Context and Driving Forces

The implementation of AI in the Chinese judicial system does not constitute an isolated technological phenomenon, but a **deliberate state strategy** articulated in the **"Fifth Judicial Reform Scheme"** of the Supreme People's Court (SPC).

> **Official objective:** Mitigate the imbalance between litigation volume and resource capacity through technological modernization, enhancing administrative efficiency and strengthening access to justice.

### 1.2 Technical Progression Timeline (1996-2030)

The Chinese judicial system has undergone **four evolutionary technical stages:**

| **Phase** | **Period** | **Characteristics** | **Functions** |
|----------|-----------|------------------|--------------|
| **1.0** | 1990s | Basic digitization | Conversion of paper files to electronic formats |
| **2.0** | 2000s | Network interconnection | Case registration and office management on digital platforms |
| **3.0** | Since 2016 | Big Data and AI | Intelligent services across the full procedural chain |
| **4.0** | Since 2021 | Ubiquitous intelligence | "Smart Court Brain" with progressive autonomy |

**Official projection:** By 2030, consolidate comprehensive technical-theoretical support system across the entire judicial process.[^1]

## Internet Courts: Innovation Laboratories

### Specialized Architecture

The Chinese system established **three specialized Internet Courts** as experimentation centers for algorithmic justice:

**Verified timeline:**
- **Hangzhou (August 2017):** First centralized digital court[^2]
- **Beijing (September 9, 2018):** Specialization in complex commercial cases[^3]
- **Guangzhou (September 28, 2018):** Jurisdiction over intellectual property and digital data[^4]

### ISSUE: What procedural protections exist in courts with massive algorithm use?

### RULE: Applicable Legal Framework

1. **SPC Opinions on Regulating AI in Judicial Fields (December 2022)**[^1]
   - Five cardinal principles: security, legality, fairness, justice, adjudication support, transparency, credibility
   - **Fundamental principle:** Explicit prohibition of delegating jurisdictional authority to machines
   - AI results have **merely referential**, non-binding character

2. **International Soft Law**
   - AAA-ICDR Principles Supporting AI in ADR (2023): Mandatory human supervision
   - CIArb Guidelines (2025): Prohibition of automated decision-making without human intervention

### APPLICATION: Implemented Protections

#### "Human + AI" Hybrid Model

Internet Courts operate under explicitly **hybrid** architecture, where:

✓ **AI assumes:** Administrative functions, predictive analysis, draft generation, evidence analysis  
✓ **Human retains:** Final decision authority, legal accountability, discretionary power

<Callout type="warning">
**Identified risk:** Extensive technology deployment could gradually erode judges' analytical capacity, documented as "deskilling" (professional deskilling).
</Callout>

## Technical System Architecture

### 2.1 The "Smart Court Brain": AI Engine Integration

The fundamental architecture rests on the so-called **"Smart Court Brain"**, a centralized computation engine designed to amalgamate:

- **AI capabilities:** Neural networks, natural language processing (NLP), machine learning
- **Data centers:** National judicial information repository
- **Support applications:** Risk analysis, outcome prediction, grounds generation

**Technical functions:**
- Creation of national judicial resource repository
- Execution of **logical inference** processes on massive databases
- Generation of predictions and substantive recommendations

### 2.2 Judicial Blockchain Infrastructure

The security "backbone" lies in **judicial blockchain (chain judicial)** platforms that:

1. **Ensure immutability:** Data conversion to hash values, synchronization across multiple nodes
2. **Verify integrity:** Real-time traceability of electronic evidence
3. **Facilitate automation:** Award execution in low-complexity cases

<Callout type="success">
**Legal advantage:** The presumption of authenticity of digital evidence on blockchain reduces the need for intermediaries and strengthens legal certainty.
</Callout>

### 2.3 Support Technologies: NLP and Voice Recognition

Daily operability sustained by:

| Technology | Precision | Application |
|-----------|-----------|-----------|
| Voice recognition (iFLYTEK) | >90% | Synchronous transcription of hearings |
| NLP | >90% | Semantic extraction of laws and regulations |
| Image analysis | Variable | Complementary assessment of statement veracity |

**Critical limitation:** Expression analysis should complement, not substitute, judicial assessment of evidence.

## Full Chain: From Registration to Execution

### 3.1 Pre-Processing Phase: Automation and Prediction

**RULE:** Civil Procedure Law Reform (2022) - Art. 5 et seq.

Initial phase automation:

- **Pleading review:** Identification of formal requirement compliance
- **Case categorization:** Automatic classification via NLP
- **Complexity prediction:** Estimated duration and necessary resources

**Verified result:** 97.8% of Chinese courts support online registration (2019)[^5]

### 3.2 Trial Assistance: Evidence Analysis

**Shanghai System 206** (Medium-complexity criminal cases)[^6]

Documented functions:
- Comprehensive visualization of evidentiary materials
- Evidence standard prescription
- Synchronous transcription with >90% precision
- **Duration reduction:** Complex cases accelerated >50%

<Callout type="info">
**Important clarification:** Evidence assessment responsibility remains linked to the human judge to safeguard fundamental rights.
</Callout>

### 3.3 Adjudication and Execution: Grounds Generation

#### Automatic Grounds Generation Framework

System employing **deep learning** algorithms to:

1. Identification of **semantic vectors** in legal facts
2. Extraction of applicable norms
3. Generation of **judgment drafts** (not final decisions)

**Verified application:** Grounds generation in jurisdictions with high-volume standardized litigation[^7]

#### Execution Phase: Asset Tracking

AI facilitates:
- Multi-platform data aggregation on debtor solvency
- Queries on bank deposits, real estate, stock assets
- Predictive analysis to mitigate execution evasion
- **Blockchain verification** to ensure traceability

## Regulatory Framework and Reforms

### 4.1 Soft Law: SPC Opinions

**Cardinal document:** "Opinions on Regulating and Strengthening Applications of Artificial Intelligence in Judicial Fields" (December 2022)[^1]

**Established regulatory principles:**

1. **Security and Legality:** Compliance with current regulations; explicit prohibition of extreme-risk applications
2. **Fairness and Justice:** Protection against algorithmic biases; guarantee of equal access
3. **Adjudication Support:** AI as assistive instrument, never decisional
4. **Transparency and Credibility:** Audit-able and explainable systems
5. **Public Order Respect:** Integrity of adversarial process; fundamental rights respect

**Official timeline:**
- 2025: Optimized functional system
- 2030: Comprehensive technical-theoretical support in full judicial process

### 4.2 Legislative Reforms: Online Litigation

**Civil Procedure Law Amendments (2022)** - Full legal effects

✓ Procedural activities on platforms have equivalent legal validity to in-person procedures  
✓ Notification via electronic means (including WeChat, messaging apps) permitted with party consent  
✓ **Voluntariness principle:** Coercive imposition of digital means prohibited

**Source:** Lee, Tsai & Partners (2021); CMS Legal (2022)[^8]

### 4.3 Regulatory Specialization: Internet Courts

**Jurisdiction Provisions (October 2025)** - Regulatory update[^9]

Refined competence over:
- Data property conflicts
- AI algorithm disputes
- Virtual property and cryptocurrencies
- Privacy in digital environments

#### Digital Evidence Authenticity on Blockchain

**Judicial presumption rule:**

> When electronic evidence has been technically verified via blockchain and its hash validated, **the court presumes its authenticity** unless counter-evidence demonstrates integrity alteration.

**Basis:** Provisions on Evidence in Civil Procedures (updated 2025)

## Critical Legal Challenges

### 5.1 The Algorithmic "Black Box" Phenomenon

#### ISSUE: How can transparency be guaranteed in AI systems whose internal logic is unobservable?

#### RULE: Fundamental Rights at Stake

- **Art. 22 GDPR (though not applicable in China, establishes standard):** Right not to be subject to solely automated decision with significant legal effects
- **Right to Defense (Universal Principle):** Ability to challenge decision grounds
- **Transparency Principle:** Access to decision reasons
- **Due Process (Chinese Law):** Right to fair trial before impartial tribunal

#### APPLICATION: Identified Risks

The **"black box" is defined** by internal logical layers whose operation cannot be directly observed by human operators.

**Legal consequences:**
1. Inability to identify and challenge specific grounds
2. Potential violation of right to defense
3. Decision-making process opacity
4. Difficulty identifying systematic biases

<Callout type="error">
**Required safeguard:** All judicial AI systems must undergo protocols for:
- Verifiability (external audit)
- Testability (functionality testing)
- Authority registration (decision logs)
</Callout>

### 5.2 Algorithmic Biases: Reproduction of Prejudices

#### ISSUE: Can AI perpetuate historical discrimination under the appearance of technical neutrality?

#### RULE: Non-Discrimination Principles

- **International Law:** International Covenant on Civil and Political Rights, Art. 26 (equality before law)
- **European Law (reference):** GDPR Art. 22 + AI Act Art. 10 (training data quality)
- **Soft Law:** AAA-ICDR Principles (2023); CIArb Guidelines (2025)

#### APPLICATION: Manifestations in Judicial Systems

| Risk | Manifestation | Impact |
|--------|---------------|--------|
| **Historical bias** | Training data contains discriminatory decisions from the past | Perpetuation of injustice under "technical objectivity" cloak |
| **Geographic bias** | Systems trained with data from developed regions | Procedural injustice in less-favored jurisdictions |
| **Gender/ethnicity bias** | Spurious correlations in data | Discriminatory decisions even without intent |
| **Garbage In, Garbage Out** | Incorporation of false legal provisions into databases | Systemic contamination of judicial results |

**Remedy:** Continuous audits of training datasets; **data governance** mechanisms to mitigate biases

### 5.3 Due Process and Deskilling Risk

#### ISSUE: How to preserve human legal reasoning upon dependence on automated suggestions?

#### RULE: Due Process Principles

- **Universal:** Trial before competent, independent, impartial tribunal
- **AI-specific:** Final human accountability; judicial discretion capacity; empathy and contextual understanding

#### APPLICATION: Identified Vulnerabilities

**1. "AI blame defense"**

Risk: Judges use algorithmic recommendations as a shield to evade accountability for unpopular or erroneous decisions.

**Protection:** Explicit mandate that judges must **articulate independent grounds**, not merely reproduce system recommendations.

**2. Deskilling: Professional Deskilling**

Excessive automation dependence may cause:
- Loss of independent critical analysis capacity
- Atrophy of specialized legal reasoning
- Degradation of professional standards

**Protection:** Mandatory training programs in **algorithmic auditing and technical comprehension** for judges.

**3. Lack of Empathy in Difficult Cases**

AI lacks:
- Understanding of complex human dynamics
- Empathy for vulnerable situations
- Moral discretion for gray areas of law

**Protection:** Restriction of algorithmic autonomy to highly standardized cases; mandatory human intervention in "hard cases."

## Conclusions and Outlook

### 6.1 Data Governance: Building Intellectual Capital

**ISSUE:** How to transition from technical experimentation to robust governance?

**CONCLUSION:** Success of Smart Courts depends less on technical architecture than on **building specialized legal talent base**.

Identified critical gaps:
- Shortage of professionals with concurrent computer science and law domain expertise
- Dependence on external technology companies
- Insufficient judicial training in system auditing

**Recommendation:** Comprehensive legal education reform integrating AI as a core discipline, training judges to **audit, not merely use**, automated tools.

### 6.2 Functional Differentiation: From Support to Controlled Autonomy

**2030 Outlook:**

#### For Standardized Cases (Low Complexity)
- Low-value claims
- Microcredits
- Transport conflicts
- **Viability:** Use of "algorithmic arbitrators" or "robot judges" as valid solution for efficiency and consistency

#### For Complex Cases (Hard Cases)
- Gray areas of law
- Multidimensional conflicts
- Fundamental rights in tension
- **Restriction:** AI remains in assistive role; mandatory critical validation by human judge

<Callout type="success">
**"Human + AI" hybrid model** allows optimizing efficiency without compromising justice system integrity.
</Callout>

### 6.3 Final Balance: Social Legitimacy of Digital Justice

**FINAL CONCLUSION:**

The future of smart justice should not aspire to total displacement of the human element, but to **consolidation of a hybrid system** where:

✓ **AI acts as instrument** to relieve administrative burden  
✓ **Human retains full authority** over judicial decision  
✓ **Transparency is non-negotiable** to maintain social legitimacy  
✓ **Human empathy guides** law application in vulnerable cases  

> Administrative efficiency, though optimized through algorithms, **can never justify sacrifice of fundamental rights** or erosion of procedural protections that have been cornerstones of rule of law for centuries.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**What is the "Smart Court Brain"?**
It is the technical core of the Chinese judicial system integrating AI engines and data centers to provide knowledge support, decision assistance, and AI services throughout the judicial process.

**Can an arbitrator or judge delegate their final decision to AI?**
No. All international guidelines (CIArb, SVAMC, AAA) and Chinese regulations (SPC) explicitly prohibit delegating decision-making authority; AI must act only as a support tool and the final result must be the work of responsible human judgment.

**What risks does AI use in "High Risk" cases under the EU?**
Under the EU AI Act, systems used in arbitration and courts are considered high-risk, requiring strict transparency measures, human supervision, data quality, and log retention for at least six months.

**How does the Chinese system guarantee authenticity of digital evidence?**
It uses judicial blockchain infrastructure that generates immutable hash values for each evidence piece, enabling synchronous verification between nodes and legal presumption of authenticity if chain data matches presented data.

**What is the "Black Box" risk?**
It refers to lack of transparency in the internal logic of deep learning algorithms, where neither developers nor judges can observe how the system arrived at a specific recommendation, affecting the parties' right to challenge legal reasoning.

**What innovations did China's 2022 Civil Procedure Law reform introduce?**
It granted full legal validity to procedural activities conducted through online platforms and expanded electronic means for document notification (such as WeChat), provided party consent exists.
