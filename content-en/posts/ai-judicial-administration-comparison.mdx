# AI in Judicial Administration: Spain-United Kingdom Comparative Analysis

## Judicial Artificial Intelligence Regulation: Divergent Models (2026)

Spain has approved **CGPJ Instruction 2/2026** (January 2026), the first binding European regulation specifically governing the use of AI systems by judges and magistrates. Simultaneously, the United Kingdom has updated its **AI Guidance for Judicial Office Holders** (October 2025), adopting a flexible, principles-based approach.

Both instruments address the same substantive challenges: algorithmic hallucinations, judicial data protection, judicial independence, and responsibility allocation. However, their regulatory strategies differ significantly.

## Key Differences in Judicial AI Regulation

### Spanish Model
Closed system with prior centralized authorization through the CGPJ, nine binding principles (human control, non-substitution, responsibility), and explicit permitted/prohibited uses. Based on the European AI Act (Annex III) and Spanish data protection regulations.

### British Model
Open approach based on individual principles and responsibility, compatible with common law tradition. Six guiding principles without direct legal consequences: understanding AI limitations, confidentiality, accuracy, bias, personal responsibility, and transparency.

## Implications for Digital Justice

This divergence has relevant practical consequences: justiciability of procedural rights, judicial accountability, and potential regulatory harmonization within the EU framework.
