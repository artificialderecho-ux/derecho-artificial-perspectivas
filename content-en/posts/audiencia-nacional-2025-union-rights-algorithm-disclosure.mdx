The National Court estimates the claim of the General Workers' Confederation (CGT) against FOUNDEVER SPAIN S.A. and declares the fundamental right of union freedom violated due to the company's refusal to provide information on the parameters, rules, and instructions of the algorithms used for the allocation of variable shifts in the Barcelona, Madrid, and Seville centers. The judgment orders the company to immediately inform in a clear and accessible manner, declares the omission conduct null, and sets compensation of 6,250 euros for moral damages. The ruling directly applies Art. 64.4(d) of the Workers' Statute and constitutional and ordinary jurisprudence on the right to union information.

### Context and Scope of the Document

This is a fundamental rights protection proceeding before the Social Chamber of the National Court. The controversy revolves around the company's information obligation regarding an algorithmic system that determines the distribution of variable shifts among personnel with rotating shifts. The company initially denied the existence of algorithms, but evidence presented (testimonial testimony and previous judgments from the same Chamber) demonstrated that the system employs a mathematical formula that processes load forecast and client needs to assign rest periods.

### Legal Grounds

The right to information of union representatives forms an essential part of the core content of union freedom (Art. 28.1 Spanish Constitution and Art. 2.2(d) LOLS). Constitutional Court Judgment 281/2005 and Supreme Court jurisprudence (including STS 6-11-2024, rec. 14/2022 and STS 954/2022) qualify it as an indispensable element for the effective exercise of union action. Art. 64.4(d) of the Workers' Statute—under the wording introduced by Law 12/2021—expressly imposes on the company the duty to inform the works council (and by extension union delegates) about "the parameters, rules, and instructions on which algorithms or artificial intelligence systems are based that affect decision-making that may impact working conditions, access to and maintenance of employment, including profile creation."
