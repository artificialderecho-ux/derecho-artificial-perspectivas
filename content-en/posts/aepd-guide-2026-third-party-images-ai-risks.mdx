The **AEPD Guide from January 2026** on using third-party images in AI systems identifies two main types of risks:

- **Visible risks**: Unauthorized dissemination, deepfakes, violation of the right to one's own image.
- **Invisible risks**: Training models with images without consent, persistent reuse, opacity in intermediate flows.

### Key GDPR obligations
- Art. 5 and 6: Lawfulness, minimization, and transparency mandatory.
- Art. 35: Data protection impact assessment (DPIA) almost always required.
- Art. 25: Privacy by design and by default in AI systems.

### Practical recommendations for controllers and providers
- Obtain explicit consent or clear lawful basis before using images.
- Implement effective anonymization and data minimization.
- Require contracts with AI providers guaranteeing no reuse.
- Conduct periodic audits of datasets and training processes.

This guide does not introduce new regulations but rigorously applies GDPR principles to the specific context of generative image AI.
