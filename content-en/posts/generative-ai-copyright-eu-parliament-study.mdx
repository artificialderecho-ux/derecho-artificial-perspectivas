# Generative AI and Copyright in the EU: European Parliament Study

Exhaustive study by the European Parliament (2025) on the challenges that generative AI poses to Copyright in the European Union. The report analyzes the misalignment between AI training practices and current text and data mining (TDM) exceptions, proposing structural reforms to protect creativity and author remuneration.

This study, commissioned by the JURI Committee of the European Parliament, examines the legal uncertainty of machine-generated content and the risks to European cultural heritage. It is a key document for understanding the direction of future legislative reforms that will complement the AI Act.

### Key Points of the Study:

* **Training and Exceptions:** Analysis of the mismatch between massive data use for AI and the exceptions under Articles 3 and 4 of the Copyright Directive.
* **Status of AI Creation:** Debate on human authorship and the protection (or lack thereof) of works generated entirely by algorithms.
* **Transparency and Opt-out:** Proposals to harmonize data exclusion mechanisms and transparency obligations for training "inputs."
* **Licensing Models:** Exploration of equitable licenses to balance technological innovation with moral and economic rights.

An indispensable resource for specialized IP firms, technology companies, and collective rights management entities seeking to anticipate the next wave of European regulation.
