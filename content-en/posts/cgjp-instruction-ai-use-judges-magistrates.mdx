# Instruction 2/2026 of the CGPJ: Use of AI by Judges and Magistrates

Analysis of Instruction 2/2026 of the CGPJ: the first detailed regulatory framework concerning the use of Artificial Intelligence by judges and magistrates in Spain. This document establishes the boundaries to ensure that AI serves as a supporting tool and never a substitute for the jurisdictional function, safeguarding fundamental rights and the principle of transparency.

Instruction 2/2026, approved by the Plenary of the General Council of the Judicial Power on January 28, 2026, marks a milestone in the digitization of Justice. Aligned with Regulation (EU) 2024/1689 (AI Act), this norm defines judicial AI systems as "high-risk" and establishes strict human oversight protocols.

### Key Aspects of the Instruction:

* **Jurisdictional Exclusivity Principle:** AI may only assist in the investigation and interpretation of facts, but the final decision rests exclusively with the judge.
* **Algorithmic Transparency:** Obligation to report which systems are used and under what parameters they were trained.
* **Data Protection:** Specific measures for the processing of personal data in the jurisdictional domain under CGPJ oversight.
* **Oversight and Control:** The CGPJ is granted monitoring authority over any AI system implemented in the courts.

This agreement is essential for attorneys and legal practitioners who need to challenge or audit resolutions where automated decision-support systems are suspected to have been involved.
