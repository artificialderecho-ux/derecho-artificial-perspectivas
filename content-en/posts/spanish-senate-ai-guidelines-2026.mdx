# Spanish Senate Approves Pioneering Guidelines for Responsible Artificial Intelligence Use

**February 16, 2026** — The Senate of Spain has approved a comprehensive regulatory framework governing the use of artificial intelligence in its constitutional, parliamentary, and administrative functions, becoming one of the first legislative institutions to establish specific guidelines for the responsible deployment of this technology.

## An Ethical and Legal Framework for Institutional AI

The **Guidelines for the Use of Artificial Intelligence in the Senate** establish the principles, procedures, and safeguards necessary to harness AI's transformative potential while minimizing the risks associated with its implementation in the parliamentary sphere.

### Main Objectives

The guidelines pursue three fundamental objectives:

1. **Improve efficiency** of citizen representation and Government oversight functions
2. **Guarantee respect** for fundamental rights and public freedoms
3. **Ensure democratic quality** through responsible and transparent use of technology

## Scope of Application

### Personal Scope

The regulation applies to:
- Senators exercising their parliamentary functions
- Civil service and contractual staff of the Senate
- Temporary staff and parliamentary group staff
- Any person working under Chamber supervision

### Material Scope

The guidelines regulate the use of AI systems in all constitutional, parliamentary, and administrative functions performed in the Senate.

## Comprehensive Risk Management

The document identifies and addresses five main risk categories:

### 1. Risks to Fundamental Rights
- Violation of privacy and data protection
- Discriminatory biases against persons or groups
- Infringement of intellectual property
- Limitation of access to public information

### 2. Operational Risks
- Generation of incorrect or misleading information (hallucinations)
- Inadequate decision-making by autonomous systems
- Institutional compromise through dissemination of erroneous content

### 3. Security Risks
- Exploitable vulnerabilities through external attacks
- Manipulation of systems, networks, or data
- Compromise of confidentiality and reliability

### 4. Institutional Risks
- Legal liability for damages to third parties
- Diminishment of institutional prestige

### 5. Environmental Impact
- Significant energy consumption of AI systems

## Guiding Principles

The guidelines are based on **15 ethical and legal principles**:

- **Responsibility** in the conscious use of risks and potentialities
- **Reliability, robustness, and security** of implemented systems
- **Respect for human autonomy** without manipulative patterns
- **Transparency** in operation and explainability of decisions
- **Proportionality** adjusted to specific needs
- **Mandatory human oversight** of all results
- **Accountability** without evasion of personal responsibility
- **Openness** and interoperability of systems
- **Privacy** and protection of personal data (GDPR/LOPDGDD)
- **Equality** and non-discrimination in algorithms
- **Regulatory compliance** with EU AI Regulation 2024/1689
- **Mandatory knowledge** of guidelines prior to use
- **Technological openness** favorable to innovation

## Regulation of Acquisition and Deployment

### Evaluation Process

Before implementing any AI system, the following is required:

1. **Impact assessment** supervised by the Information Security Commission
2. **Detailed technical documentation** (architecture, data, algorithms, risk mitigation)
3. **Compliance with** specific institutional standards

### Procurement Criteria

Tender specifications must:
- Incorporate all ethical and legal principles
- Positively value ISO/IEC 42001 certification
- Include clauses on data protection, equality, and copyright
- Require providers to demonstrate good practices in ethical development

### Training Data Protection

**Fundamental guarantee:** Providers must ensure that data introduced by Senate users **is not used to train any AI model**.

## Practical Use Guidelines

### Authorized Systems

- Only **expressly authorized** systems may be used by the Senate
- Absolute prohibition of unauthorized tools on institutional equipment or networks
- Updated list available on the intranet

### Sensitive Information Protection

**Absolute prohibitions**:
- Information subject to **parliamentary secrecy**: it will never be introduced into any AI system
- Information subject to **parliamentary reserve** or protected by intellectual property: only in authorized non-public systems and with prior superior hierarchical authorization

### Mandatory Oversight

- **Human verification** prior to official use of all AI-generated information or decisions
- Special attention to rights violations and institutional functioning
- The human supervisor's personal responsibility is inescapable

### Citizen Transparency

The Senate Transparency Portal will publish:
- AI systems used in the Chamber
- Specific purposes of each system
- Basic operation of tools

## Training and Capacity Building

### Continuing Education Plan

Aimed at all persons subject to the guidelines to ensure:
- Understanding of risks and limitations
- Knowledge of capabilities and functionalities
- Responsible and effective use of systems

### Technical Specialization

Specialized training for profiles in:
- Information and Communications Technologies Directorate
- Other directorates according to specific needs

## Governance and Responsibility

### Competent Bodies

**Direct responsibility:**
- Information and Communications Technologies Directorate
- In collaboration with the Data Protection Officer

**Strategic supervision:**
- Senate Bureau
- General Secretariat and Deputy General Secretariat for Administrative Affairs
- Information Security Commission

### Sanctioning Regime

Non-compliance may result in **disciplinary responsibility** pursuant to the regime established by the Senate Bureau within six months.

## Monitoring Reports

The General Secretariat will prepare an **annual report** including:
- Specific uses of AI systems
- Utilization statistics
- Evaluation of implemented systems
- Recorded incidents
- Implemented improvements

This report will be:
1. Presented to the Information Security Commission
2. Elevated to the Chamber Bureau
3. Published on the Transparency Portal (as appropriate)

## Entry into Force and Transition Period

- **Entry into force:** 60 days after publication in the Official Bulletin of the Cortes Generales
- **Adaptation of existing systems:** 6 months from entry into force

## Reference Legal Framework

The guidelines are based on:

- **Regulation (EU) 2024/1689** on Artificial Intelligence
- **GDPR** (General Data Protection Regulation 2016/679)
- **LOPDGDD** (Organic Law 3/2018 on Data Protection)
- Senate Regulations
- Complementary national legislation

## Conclusion

With these guidelines, the Senate positions itself at the institutional forefront in regulating the use of artificial intelligence, establishing a governance model that balances:

✓ **Technological innovation** for greater efficiency  
✓ **Fundamental rights protection**  
✓ **Democratic transparency** in AI use  
✓ **Institutional responsibility** and accountability  

This regulatory framework guarantees that the Senate harnesses AI's potential to improve service to citizens, while preserving the democratic values, fundamental rights, and human dignity that underpin our Rule of Law.
