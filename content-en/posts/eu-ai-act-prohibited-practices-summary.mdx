# EU AI Act: 8 Prohibited AI Practices - What You Must Know as a Lawyer

## Status: Effective February 2, 2025

Since **February 2, 2025**, the European Union has **8 completely prohibited artificial intelligence practices** according to the EU AI Act (Regulation 2024/1689).

This is not "flexible regulation." It is not "recommended guidance." They are **direct prohibitions** that:
- ✅ Apply immediately
- ✅ To everyone (public and private sectors)
- ✅ Throughout the EU
- ✅ With sanctions up to **6% of annual global revenue**

---

## The 8 Prohibited Practices Summarized

| # | Prohibited | Applies to | What Exactly? |
|---|-----------|----------|---|
| **1** | Harmful manipulation | Everyone | Subliminal/deceptive techniques that alter behavior |
| **2** | Exploitation of vulnerabilities | Everyone | Exploit age/disability/social situation to alter behavior |
| **3** | Citizen scoring | Everyone | System that evaluates social behavior + generates consequences in other contexts |
| **4** | Crime prediction (profiling) | Everyone | Predict crime based SOLELY on person profiling |
| **5** | Mass facial extraction | Everyone | Create facial recognition database from internet/CCTV without filter |
| **6** | Emotion recognition | Workplaces + Schools | Deduce emotions of employee/student for evaluation |
| **7** | Sensitive biometric categorization | Everyone | Use biometric data to classify race/religion/sexuality |
| **8** | Real-time facial identification | Public authorities | Mass facial recognition in public spaces (with limited exceptions) |

---

## What's PROHIBITED for Lawyers/Legal Firms?

### Prohibition 1 & 2: Manipulation and Exploitation

❌ **Prohibited:**
- AI system that presents information in a deliberately misleading way
- Algorithm that exploits isolation of vulnerable client to manipulate decision
- Subliminal techniques to alter behavior of a party

✅ **Permitted:**
- Clear presentation of options (even if favoring one)
- Honest advertising/marketing of services
- Lawful persuasion

### Prohibition 3: Citizen Scoring

❌ **Prohibited:**
```
System that evaluates client and generates restrictions:
"Client X: low score → reject"
"Client X: low score → require extra guarantee"
Based on social profiling, not verifiable facts
```

✅ **Permitted:**
```
"Client X has no payment capacity → require guarantee"
(Based on verifiable financial information)
```

### Prohibition 4: Crime Prediction by Profiling

❌ **Prohibited:**
- AI that predicts "John will likely commit a crime"
- Based SOLELY on:
  - Demographic profile
  - Personality characteristics
  - Family history
  - Without objective facts

✅ **Permitted:**
- AI that analyzes "John was arrested 3 times for theft"
- And helps assess risk based on objective FACTS
- But: Final decision (bail, sentence) requires human

### Prohibition 5: Mass Facial Extraction

❌ **Prohibited:**
```
Activity 1: Social media web scraping
→ Extract ALL faces
→ Create database
PROHIBITED

Activity 2: Install camera in office
→ Extract ALL faces
→ Without selective consent
PROHIBITED
```

✅ **Permitted:**
```
Selective search:
Search for specific face of missing person in CCTV
(Targeted search, not mass)
```

### Prohibition 6: Emotion Recognition (Workplaces/Schools)

❌ **Prohibited:**
- AI system that deduces "stress" from employee
- AI system that deduces "lack of attention" from student
- To evaluate performance
- Even with "consent" (considered coercive)

✅ **Permitted:**
- Psychology clinic that deduces emotion for diagnosis (with real consent)
- System that detects person in crisis for security

### Prohibition 7: Sensitive Biometric Categorization

❌ **Prohibited (without exceptions):**
- Use biometric data to deduce/classify:
  - Race
  - Political opinions
  - Religion
  - Sexual life
  - Sexual orientation

✅ **Permitted:**
- Everything else

### Prohibition 8: Real-time Facial Identification (Mainly for Authorities)

❌ **Prohibited:**
- Police using mass facial recognition on street
- Without prior judicial authorization

✅ **Permitted (with restrictions):**
- Specifically search for crime victim
- Prevent imminent terrorist attack
- Locate serious crime suspect
- REQUIRES: PRIOR judicial authorization + notification

## What's PERMITTED for Lawyers/Firms?

### Allowed AI Uses

✅ **Allowed to use AI for:**

1. **Legal research**
   - Search case law
   - Analyze precedents
   - Research regulations

2. **Document organization**
   - Categorize documents
   - Extract relevant information
   - Summarize content

3. **Decision support**
   - AI that helps evaluate case
   - BUT: If based on verifiable objective facts
   - BUT: Final decision requires lawyer

4. **Risk analysis**
   - Evaluate case risk
   - Based on verifiable information
   - Not on person profiling

5. **Case law prediction**
   - AI that predicts "similar case was decided this way"
   - As analysis tool
   - To inform strategy

## What Must You Do Right Now? (Immediate Actions)

### Week 1: Audit

```
CHECKLIST:
☐ Does my firm use AI in any way?
☐ For what tasks?
☐ What tools? (ChatGPT, Claude, Lexis+AI, etc.)
☐ How is it used? (Analysis, research, client?, etc.)

FOR EACH USE:
☐ Does it comply with 8 prohibitions Art. 5?
  - Manipulation? NO
  - Exploitation? NO
  - Citizen scoring? NO
  - Crime prediction? NO
  - Facial data? NO
  - Emotions? NO
  - Sensitive biometric data? NO
  - Facial identification? NO

If answer ALL = NO → PERMITTED
If answer ANY = YES → PROHIBITED (cease immediately)
```

### Week 2-3: Governance

```
CREATE FIRM AI POLICY:
1. Permitted uses (explicit list)
2. Prohibited uses (explicit list)
3. Approved/disapproved tools
4. Who approves new uses
5. Required documentation
6. Mandatory training
```

### Month 1-2: Implementation

```
ACTIONS:
☐ Train legal team on EU AI Act Art. 5
☐ Cease any prohibited use immediately
☐ If using third-party tools: Request compliance certification
☐ Document all permitted uses (to demonstrate compliance)
☐ Update client engagement terms (transparency)
```

## Sanctions and Enforcement

### What Happens If You Non-Compliance

**Administrative fines:**
- Up to **6% of annual global revenue** (serious case)
- Or up to **€30,000,000** (whichever is greater)

**Other consequences:**
- Prohibition to operate in EU
- System confiscation
- Civil liability
- Reputational damage

### Who Enforces

- **National authorities** (Spain: mainly AEPD)
- **Market surveillance authorities**
- **European Commission**

Enforcement has begun. This is not a future threat—it is present.

## Key Questions: Interpretation

### Can I use public ChatGPT?

**Depends on use:**
- ✅ For research/legal analysis: YES (if not processing client sensitive data)
- ❌ To analyze case with sensitive data without safeguards: NO (GDPR + Art. 5)
- ✅ For help drafting: YES (if you verify output before use)

### Is my LegalTech provider responsible?

**Shared responsibility:**
- Provider: Responsible for design compliant with Art. 5
- Firm: Responsible for use
- If provider violates + firm uses: BOTH can be sanctioned

### Does client consent cure non-compliance?

**NO in unacceptable risk cases:**
- Consent does not allow what's prohibited
- Even if client agrees to use AI that violates Art. 5 → still prohibited

### Is it retroactive?

**For Art. 5 (prohibitions): NOT retroactive**
- From Feb 2, 2025 it's mandatory
- Uses before: Not subject to Art. 5
- But: Data transfers to USA via AI are GDPR-prohibited always

## Conclusion: How to Proceed

**The reality:**
- EU AI Act prohibits 8 practices effectively since days ago
- Compliance is mandatory—not optional
- Firm audits are occurring/will occur

**Recommended actions:**
1. Immediate audit of any AI use
2. Cease immediately any prohibited use
3. Create clear firm policy
4. Train team
5. Select compliant tools
6. Document to demonstrate compliance

**Bottom line:**

The EU AI Act does not prohibit AI. It prohibits **certain uses incompatible with fundamental rights**.

You can use AI in legal practice. But you must do so knowing exactly what's allowed and what's not.
