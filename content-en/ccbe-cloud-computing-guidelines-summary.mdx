# CCBE Guidelines on the Use of Cloud Computing by Lawyers (Summary)

This summary captures the essential points of the CCBE 2025 Guidelines on the use of cloud computing services by lawyers and law firms:

- They reinforce confidentiality and technical competence obligations as pillars of compliance.
- They integrate GDPR, NIS2, AI Act, and cybersecurity regulations into a single operational framework.
- They propose a set of ten recommendations covering risk analysis, regulatory compliance, information security, provider evaluation, data processing location, data processing, business continuity, and insurance coverage.
- They clarify that technological outsourcing never exempts the attorney from professional responsibility over client data.
